  • What is this?

  • Brid.gy copies comments, likes, and reshares from social networks to your web site. If you post links to your site on social networks (aka POSSEing to the silos), and people interact with those links, Brid.gy backfeeds those interactions to your site. You can see an example here.

    The first version of Brid.gy was released a couple years ago, but only supported backfeeding comments to WordPress. This version is better, thanks to webmentions and the inspiration of the IndieWeb community.

  • How does it work?

  • When you add one of your accounts, Brid.gy checks it periodically for posts and comments on those posts. When it finds a new comment on a post, it looks for links to your web site in the post, then sends webmentions to those links. It also translates the comment to microformats2 HTML so that your web site can fetch and parse it.

    Brid.gy is open source. It's written in Python and runs on Google App Engine. It uses oauth-dropins to let users connect to the silos, activitystreams-unofficial to fetch data from the silos and convert it to a standard format, and webmention-tools to send webmentions.

  • How much does it cost?

  • Nothing! I have a great day job, and Brid.gy is small, so thanks to App Engine, it doesn't cost much to run. I don't need donations, promise.

    If you really want to contribute, jump into the code and send me a pull request, or feel free to donate to the ASPCA or the EFF in my name.

  • Which things does Brid.gy send, specifically?

  • Brid.gy detects and sends webmentions for:

    • Twitter @-replies, retweets, and favorites of your tweets.
    • Your Twitter favorites of other people's tweets.
    • Facebook comments and likes on your posts...but not reshares of them, unfortunately. I haven't yet found a way to get those from Facebook's API.
    • Google+ comments, +1s, and reshares of your posts.
    • Instagram comments and likes on your pictures.

  • Someone commented (or liked or reshared) my post, but Brid.gy hasn't seen it yet!

  • Brid.gy doesn't work in realtime; it just checks your accounts for new activity every 15 minutes or so. Twitter favorites are the one exception; Brid.gy usually sees and handles those within seconds. It may expand to handle more data in realtime in the future from more of the sites, but it will probably never be 100%.

  • Brid.gy is ignoring one of my Twitter replies!

  • Does that reply include an @-mention of the user it's replying to? Twitter's API doesn't have a way to get a tweet's replies, so Brid.gy searches for @-mentions of the tweet's author to find its replies. That means it can't find replies that omit the @-mention.

  • What are all these brid-gy.appspot.com links in the comments that Brid.gy sends to my site?

  • Short answer: your server's webmention code is probably rendering the webmention source URL. It should instead use the source page's microformats2 u-url property. This is a common oversight, since they're usually the same, but Brid.gy is a special snowflake. :P You might want to file a bug against your server, or even fix it yourself. Here's an example discussion.

    Longer answer: Webmentions don't include the actual content of the comment or like or reshare, just a link to it. The recipient fetches that link and extracts the content and other data using microformats2. The social networks don't (yet) support microformats2, so Brid.gy translates their data to microformats2 HTML and serves those translations as webmention targets so that recipients can fetch and parse them.

  • OK, fine. But why brid-gy.appspot.com instead of brid.gy?

  • It's an SSL thing. The brid.gy domain fully supports and uses SSL, but it uses SNI, a newer SSL feature that some older clients and libraries have trouble with. Modern browsers are generally fine, but when Brid.gy sends a webmention, the server that receives it also needs to understand SNI, and some still don't. appspot.com's SSL doesn't use SNI, so we use it for webmention sources in order to be as compatible as possible.

  • What do you do with my data?

  • Nothing! Brid.gy isn't a business, and never will be, so I don't have the same motivations to abuse your data that other services might. More concretely, I won't ever send you email, I store as little of your PII (personally identifiable information) as possible, and I never have access to any of your passwords.

  • What is this?

  • Brid.gy backfeeds comments from social networks to your web site. If you post links on social networks to pages on your own site (aka POSSEing to the silos), and people comment on those links, Brid.gy copies the comments back to your site. You can see an example here.

    The first version of Brid.gy was released a couple years ago, but only supported backfeeding comments to WordPress. This version is better, thanks to webmentions and the inspiration of the IndieWeb community.

  • Who are you? Why did you make this?

  • I'm Ryan Barrett. I'm just a guy who likes the web and likes owning his own data.

  • How does it work?

  • When you add one of your accounts, Brid.gy checks it periodically for posts and comments on those posts. When it finds a new comment on a post, it looks for links to your web site in the post, then sends webmentions to those links. It also translates the comment to microformats2 HTML so that your web site can fetch and parse it.

    Brid.gy is open source. It's written in Python and runs on Google App Engine. It uses oauth-dropins to let users connect to the silos, activitystreams-unofficial to fetch data from the silos and convert it to a standard format, and webmention-tools to send webmentions.

  • How much does it cost?

  • Nothing! I have a great day job, and Brid.gy is small, so thanks to App Engine, it doesn't (yet) cost anything to run. I don't need donations, promise.

    If you really want to contribute, jump into the code and send me a pull request, or feel free to donate to the ASPCA or the EFF in my name.

  • What do you do with my data?

  • Nothing! Brid.gy isn't a business, and never will be, so I don't have the same motivations to abuse your data that other services might. More concretely, I won't ever send you email, I store as little of your PII (personally identifiable information) as possible, and I never have access to any of your passwords.

  • Brid.gy is ignoring one of my Twitter replies!

  • Does that reply include an @-mention of the user it's replying to? Twitter's API doesn't have a way to get a tweet's replies, so Brid.gy searches for @-mentions of the tweet's author to find its replies. That means it can't find replies that omit the @-mention.
